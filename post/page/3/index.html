Biscotti

published on August 29, 2024
“Biscotti” is the plural form of biscotto. The word originates from the medieval Latin word biscoctus, meaning “twice-cooked.” It defined oven baked goods that were baked twice, so they were very dry and could be stored for long periods of time. In modern Italian, the word biscotti refers to any cookie or cracker, just as does the British use of the word “biscuit”. The number of bakings or hardness is not relevant to the term. In America, the term “biscotti” refers only to the specific Italian cookie. Read More...
