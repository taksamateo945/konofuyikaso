Movies

Rufus Sewell has appeared in over 40 movies since his debut in Twenty-One (1991).

He has shown his versatility and range by playing various characters, from villains to heroes, from historical figures to fictional ones.

Some of his most memorable movies are:

  • Carrington (1995): He played the painter Mark Gertler, who was in love with the writer Dora Carrington, played by Emma Thompson.
  • Hamlet (1996): He played Fortinbras, the prince of Norway, who claims the throne of Denmark after the death of Hamlet, played by Kenneth Branagh.
  • Dangerous Beauty (1998): He played Marco Venier, a Venetian nobleman who falls in love with a courtesan, played by Catherine McCormack.
  • Dark City (1998): He played John Murdoch, a man who wakes up with no memory and discovers that he is living in a city controlled by a mysterious group of beings called the Strangers.
  • A Knight’s Tale (2001): He played Count Adhemar, the ruthless rival of William Thatcher, a peasant who pretends to be a knight, played by Heath Ledger.
  • The Illusionist (2006): He played Crown Prince Leopold, the heir to the Austro-Hungarian Empire, who is engaged to Sophie, the love interest of a magician, played by Edward Norton.
  • Judy (2019): He played Sid Luft, the third husband and manager of Judy Garland, played by Renée Zellweger.
  • The Father (2020): He played Paul, the son-in-law of Anthony, an elderly man who suffers from dementia, played by Anthony Hopkins.
