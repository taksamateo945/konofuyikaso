Chris Olave puts a stunning 40-time at the NFL Scouting Combine 2022

The quarterbacks and pass-catchers took center stage on Thursday during the first night of workouts at the 2022 NFL Scouting Combine. While some wide receivers and tight ends declined to participate in all of the physical tests, those who did impressed evaluators with their speed in the 40-yard dash, quickness in shuttle drills, and explosiveness in the broad and vertical jumps.

Chris Olave was the best wide receiver in Thursday's second group of wideouts, and it wasn't even close. Since his arrival at Ohio State, he's been labeled a smooth mover, and he didn't do anything to change that at the combine. Olave collected practically everything sent his way, catching numerous deep and short tosses well away from his frame. His 40-yard dash time of 4.39 seconds was more impressive than his 32-inch vertical and 10-foot broad jump.

NFL Draft prospect Chris Olave ran the 40-yard sprint and he did it quickly. The Ohio State wide receiver ran a 4.26-second 40-yard sprint in his first attempt (unofficial). Olave's mark of 4.26 seconds would tie him with Jerome Mathisand Dri Archer for the third-fastest 40-yard sprint finish in NFL Combine history. In 2017, John Ross III established a new world record of 4.22 seconds. The unofficial time was later rounded up to 4.39 seconds, much to the irritation of fans.

Olave had already been projected as a first-round pick before his performance at the NFL Combine, and he's done nothing to undermine his status in Indianapolis. The 6' 3/8" wideout from OSU weighs 187 pounds and has 9 1/2" hands.
